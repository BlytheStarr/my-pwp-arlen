About Hermit Crab Customs:

Modifying electronics has been a hobby of mine since I was a young child. I started by just taking things apart to see how they worked and seeing if I could put them back together again. But I soon started spray painting my game controllers. When I went over to my friend's houses, I could tell my controller apart from everyone else. I gained considerable interest in Gameboy advance modifications after one of my friends gave me his beat-up sp to play pokemon sapphire. The shell was pretty beat up, and the buttons were less than responsive. Hence, a few internet searches later, I had replacement parts ordered. Armed with a cheap screwdriver and youtube, I took the SP entirely apart. I started the process of putting it back together. That gifted SP worked for a while, but its small size wasn't conducive to long play sessions; the original Gameboy had a better layout, but unfortunately, the screen wasn't backlit. So I took the screen out of my SP and successfully modified an original Gameboy to accept the upgraded backlit screen.
