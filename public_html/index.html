About Hermit Crab Customs:

Modifying electronics has been a hobby of mine since I was a young child. I started by just taking things apart to see how they worked and seeing if I could put them back together again. But I soon started spray-painting my game controllers. When I went over to my friends' houses, I could tell my controller apart from everyone else. I gained considerable interest in Game Boy Advance modifications after one of my friends gave me his beat-up SP to play Pokemon sapphire. The shell was pretty damaged, and the buttons were less than responsive. A few internet searches later, I had replacement parts ordered. Armed with a cheap screwdriver and YouTube, I took the SP entirely apart. I started the process of putting it back together. That gifted SP worked for a while, but its small size wasn't conducive to long play sessions; the original Game boy had a better layout, but unfortunately, the screen wasn't backlit. So I took the screen out of my SP and successfully modified an original Game boy to accept the upgraded backlit screen. High-end knives are another passion of mine, the lack of electronics makes scale swaps and hardware replacements quite easy.

Reshelling is the act of replacing the entire shell of a device. It can be handy in repairing a worn-down device that needs to be "refreshed." A re-shell can also help to distinguish devices and give them some personalized flair.

Sometimes retro hardware is simply the best way to run retro software. Still, we have been spoiled by the screens of our modern devices, which offer better visibility in low light, and just plain better image quality. A screen swap can keep most of the original hardware intact but make the device much more straightforward and enjoyable to use.

When a childhood console has been well-loved, it can hold significant memories. Still, the first Gameboy model was released 32 years ago, and most are starting to show their age. Luckily aftermarket parts are still widely available, which means some simple swaps can keep your childhood console running smooth!
